A NEW ADVENTURE IN A ICE CREAM

Food colouring is sometimes added in addition to stabilizers.
The mixture is cooled below the freezing point of water and stirred to incorporate air spaces and prevent detectable ice crystals from forming.
It can also be made by whisking a flavoured cream base and liquid nitrogen together.
The result is a smooth, semi-solid foam that is solid at very low temperatures (below 2 °C or 35 °F). It becomes more malleable as its temperature increases.
